1979
1994

Aged 15

April: silverchair enter and win a national band competition called ‘Pick Me’ using a demo of their song ‘Tomorrow”.

September: ‘Tomorrow’ is released as a single and goes number one. From late October it spends 6 weeks at number 1 in Australia.
1995

Aged 15/16

Jan– December: The band embarks on their first tour in support of the impending release of their debut album. The tour runs through until December of 1995 and includes stops in Australia, US, UK, Canada, NZ and Europe.

March: The bands debut album, Frogstomp, is released, it charts at number 1 in Australia and breaks the top 10 of the US Billboard 200.

June: ‘Tomorrow’ is released to the United States market and peaks at number one on the Billboard Mordern Rock Tracks and Album rock Tracks charts.

December: The band perform on Saturday Night Live, making them amongst the youngest performers to ever appear on the show.

1996

Aged 16/17

Janurary: A murder case in the US draws unwanted attention when the defense counsel for two teenage defendants claims that the lyrics to the song "Israel's Son" from Frogstomp contributed to the crime. The band's manager issus a statement of condemnation, and the prosecutors reject the defense's claims.

May: The band record their second studio album, Freakshow and continue to tour throughout 1996. .

1997

Aged 17/18

February: Freakshow is released and charts at Number 1 in Australia.

February – December: The band tour extensively in support of Freakshow. Playing shows in Australia, US, UK, Canada, NZ, Europe and the Philippines. It is during this period that Daniel starts to experience the onset of his depression.

1998

Aged 18/19

The band take a scheduled break from touring to work on their third album. During this period Daniel retreats from public life to his family home in Newcastle, eventually developing Anorexia Nervosa. He later disclosed that he got down to about 50kgs at his lightest, was blacking out regularly, would take his dog, Sweep, on 3 hour walks of a night time to lose weight and was ultimately told by his family GP that if he didn’t change what he was doing to his body he would die. Whilst on break Daniel writes the bands third record, Neon Ballroom, a change to previous records that had been a collaboration between Daniel and Ben.
1999

Aged 19/20

Neon Ballroom is released in March and the band embark on a year long tour encompassing Australia, US, UK, Canada, NZ and Europe. Daniel starts dating his eventual wife, Natalie Imbruglia.
2000

Aged 20/21

The band announce their first hiatus, with Daniel unsure if wants to continue with silverchair. Daniel and Paul Mac release their first collaborative experimental project ‘I Can’t Believe It’s Not Rock’. They release a self titled that is available to steam for free on their website. Silverchair play one show that year, Falls Festival on New Year’s Eve.
2001

Aged 21/22

Janurary: The band play their only show of 2001, the Rock in Rio festival in brazil to a crowd of 250,000 people, reigniting their passion for the band. Daniel begins writing the groups fourth studio album.

June: The band enter the studio to begin record what will become Diorama. Daniel formally takes up post as co-producer. This would be the first of many collaborations between Johns and Van Dyke Parks. During the recording process Daniel is diagnosed with a rare but serious case of reactive arthritis. The arthritis left him bedridden and paralysed on his left-hand side. Johns linked the illness to severe stress from fame, his battle with anorexia, and underlying viral infections, with anxiety worsening flare-ups.
2002

Aged 22/23

March: Diorama is released. The band intend to tour extensively in support of the record but can’t due to the onset and severity of Daniel’s reactive arthritis. 2002: The band play 12 shows in total across Australia and NZ before tour plans are cancelled, an exception was made for a performance at the ARIA awards in October. Daniel undergoes intensive treatment including physiotherapy, use of a hyperbaric chamber and copious methods of pain management. It is around this time that Daniel makes a suicide pact with his brother, Heath, that if his arthritis doesn’t start to improve and Heath continues to struggle with finding work they’d take their lives together. Daniel and Natalie are engaged.
2003

Aged 23/24

March – June: The band embark on the Across The Night tour, 39 shows across Australia, NZ, UK, US, Brazil, Canada and Europe. Following this tour, the band would go on a second hiatus until 2005.

December: Daniel and Natalie wed on New Year's. The two tying the knot in Northern Queensland at the Thala Beach resort.
2004

Aged 24/25

April: Daniel and Paul Mac release their first and only record as the Dissociatives.
2005

Aged 25/26

Janurary: silverchair reform to play WaveAide – a concert staged to raise funds to support the tsunami victims of the Indian Ocean earthquake in 2004. They rediscover their spark and reform again.

Late 2005: The band announce they will be releasing a fifth studio album, Young Modern, the moniker Van Dyke Parks gave to Daniel during the record of Diorama. .

2006

Aged 26/27

The band write and record their fifth and final studio album. The band play 8 shows in Australia to fund the album.
2007

Aged 27/28

March: The band release Young Modern.

August – October: The band join forces with Powderfinger and a show of support of for Reconciliation Australia. The tour spanned Australia and NZ with many of the 33 stops being in regional areas.

The band also embark on a worldwide tour in support of Young Modern, 110 dates across Australia, US, Canada, UK, NZ and Europe.
2008

Aged 28/29

Jan-Feb: The band play the Big Day Out festival in Australia. Daniel and Natalie announce they were divorcing. Daniel begins dating Australia model, Louise Van der Vost

June: Daniel and Luke Steele begin work on their collaboration, Dreams.
2009

Aged 29/30

The band enter the studio to start work on their sixth studio album but the band experiences disunity, this album would never be released. Daniel moves to New York with Louise.
2010

Aged 30/31

The band play shows in Australia for the Groovin’ the Moo festival, the final show on May 22nd would eventually be their last appearance as a band.
2011

Aged 31/32

The band officially announce an ‘indefinite hibernation’ in May.
2012

Aged 32/33

Daniel largely retreated from the public eye, describing a "four-year protest" in the studio where he intentionally "got really weird" with his music. He also composed "Atlas," a commercial piece of music used as a jingle for Qantas, working with the Australian Chamber Orchestra. He also split with his partner Louise van der Vorst and began a new relationship with Estelita Huijer.
2013

Aged 33/34

Daniel begins work on his first solo release, Talk, to be released in 2015. He performs in Adelaide with Kimbra as special guests of Van Dyke Parks and continued to work on music for the film My Mind’s own Melody with Josh Wakely.
2014

Aged 34/35

Daniel collaborates with 360 on the track “Impossible” and appeared in its music video, his first time in filmed media since 2007. He also pleaded guilty to a mid-range drink driving charge in November and was ordered to attend a traffic offenders’ program. He continues working on his debut solo album. In March he release a four track EP titled “Aerial Love.”
2015

Aged 35/36

May: Talk is released, and Daniel is quoted as saying he would not get silverchair back together for a million bucks even if he was being held at gunpoint.

July: Daniel is hospitalised after falling outside a Sydney Bar.
2016

Aged 36/37

Daniel works as a music producer, director, and arranger for the first season of the Netflix animated series Beat Bugs, and records a cover of the Beatles' "I Am the Walrus" for the show's soundtrack. Daniel and Estelita confirm their break and he begins dating Michelle Leslie. In December of 2016, Daniel was spotted shirtless and stumbling on the streets of Sydney.
2017

Aged 37/38

Beat Bugs is released and he receives an Emmy award for his work on the project. He also receives a Grammy award for co-writing the Flume hit ‘Say it’ featuring Tove Lo, making him the only Australian to win both an Emmy and a Grammy in the same year.
2018

Aged 38/39

Daniel and Luke release their debut single as DREAMS. They also perform at Vivid Live in Sydney.
2019

Aged 39/40

He ends his relationship with Michelle Leslie. On the 11th of August The Sunday Telegraph and Daily Mail publish a false story alleging Daniel had been spending his time at S&M brother in Sydney called The Kastle. He denied the allgations and sued Newscorp for defamation. They paid him a settlement of $470,00 and both publications issued a retraction and apology.
2021

Aged 42/43

Daniel is part of the ‘Who is Daniel Johns?’ podcast. He announces his second solo album FutureNever.
2022

Aged 43/44

Daniel releases FutureNever in April, it tops the ARIA charts. He also releases his podcast and a three part YouTube series. In March, Daniel is involved in a drink-driving incident in NSW, veering onto the wrong side of a highway and colliding head-on with another vehicle. He is arrested and charged and immediately checks himself in a rehab facility upon release. Johns said he had been medicating with alcohol to deal with his PTSD, anxiety and depression, and he would be admitting himself to a rehabilitation centre. On 7 July 2022, he was sentenced. The judge said the charge was serious, but Johns' background and circumstances were exceptional, and gave him a relatively light punishment by disqualifying him from driving for seven months and a 10-month intensive corrections order.
2025

Aged 45/46

Daniel Johns continues his solo career and sparks reunion speculation after making a brief appearance at an Adelaide band's Silverchair tribute soundcheck in August, which he captioned with the mysterious message "It's happening". He also announces live "In Conversation" appearances for late 2025, where he discussed his career.
